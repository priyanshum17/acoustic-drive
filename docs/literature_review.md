# Literature Review: High-Speed Auditory Navigation

This project is grounded in established Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) research regarding non-visual data exploration. The following theories and technologies provide the baseline for the Acoustic Drive System.

## 1. Spearcons (Speech-Based Earcons)
Developed primarily by **Bruce Walker** at the **Georgia Tech Sonification Lab**, Spearcons are a fundamental innovation in auditory list navigation.

*   **Theory**: Spearcons are created by time-compressing spoken phrases (text-to-speech) until they are no longer recognizable as speech but maintain a unique acoustic signature.
*   **Key Finding**: Research shows that Spearcons outperform traditional Earcons (tonal/musical) and Auditory Icons (representational sounds) in terms of speed, accuracy, and ease of learning.
*   **Application**: They provide a way to rapidly "glance" through audio data without the overhead of natural language processing.

## 2. Auditory Skimming and Shuttling
The metaphor of "shuttling" through data (derived from editing controls in early film and audio tape machines) has been adapted for screen readers.

*   **Theory**: Navigation is not a series of discrete "jumps" but a continuous fluid movement through a spatial or serialized dataset.
*   **Research**: Studies on "Audio Skimming" emphasize the need for "Auditory Landmarks"—brief, recognizable sounds that signify structural boundaries (like the start of a new letter in a directory).
*   **Efficiency**: Shuttling allows users to estimate distance and density through temporal duration, rather than counting individual items.

## 3. Adaptive Granularity & Verbosity
Research into "Cognitive Load in Auditory Menus" (Brewster et al.) highlights the limits of auditory bandwidth.

*   **Adaptive Feedback**: Systems that dynamically switch between high-fidelity speech (slow) and low-fidelity abstraction (fast) lead to higher user performance.
*   **Verbosity Control**: The ability to mutate the level of detail based on interaction velocity is a core strategy for expert power-users of AT (Assistive Technology).
